About Project

Abstract

Student performance is influenced by learning styles, study habits, and external factors. This project analyzes a Student Performance & Learning Style Dataset using Pandas Profiling to explore patterns in academic scores, learning preferences, and study behavior. The goal is to identify how different learning styles affect student success.

Introduction

Every student has a unique learning style—some learn better through visual materials, others through listening or hands-on activities. This project studies how learning preferences impact academic performance. By analyzing the dataset, we can understand which study methods
lead to higher success rates.

Literature Survey

Studies show that students perform better when they adopt learning styles that match their cognitive strengths. Research categorizes learning
styles as visual, auditory, and kinesthetic. Factors like study hours, revision methods, and teacher interactions also influence academic
outcomes. Using data analysis, we can uncover the relationship between learning styles and academic scores.

EDA

EDA helps in understanding learning trends. Pandas Profiling is used to analyze:
• Learning styles – Identifying if students prefer visual, auditory, or kinesthetic learning.
• Study habits – How study hours and revision techniques affect performance.
• Subject-wise performance – Understanding which subjects students excel in.
• Class participation – Impact of classroom engagement on grades.
• Correlations – Finding relationships between learning styles, study habits, and academic scores.
